| The Adventure |
  • “Someone who really wants to get in a little over his head and make a dent in the universe.” - Steve Jobs

The quote from the late and greatly missed Steve Jobs has been something I have always related to and find myself fulfilling through all of life’s endeavors.

My journey is unlike any other. Growing up with my grandmother I was a quiet kid who followed directions and never had trouble with school. In high school I had many interests and thought my life could go in many different directions; engineer, entrepreneur, architect, chef, and the list goes on. Oh boy, I was right in the fact that my life would be unique.

I won the first full-tuition scholarship to attend Scottsdale Culinary Institute in a state-wide competition in Arizona. I graduated with my culinary degree. I was a chef and pastry chef at 4 star restaurants. But I was not satisfied and knew I was meant to do more.

I went back to school for business. I attended nationally top ranked Entrepreneurship and Business Management programs at the University of Arizona where I double majored. I was a General Manager and things were good, but too easy. Again, I was not satisfied and knew I was meant to do more.

God called me into the military and I enlisted in the U.S. Army as an Information Technology Specialist. I had always tinkered around with gadgets; like tube televisions, gameboys, pagers, POTS phones, and so on… I thought this was something that should be more than a hobby. I instantly excelled, deployed, lead soldiers, and gained many technical certifications. I switched from a soldier to an officer. I was selected to the Cyber Corps, and learned even more. Then I became a developer and certified as a Cyberspace Capabilities Developer. That is where I finally felt I was doing what I was called to do all along.

Being in cyber as a developer is a culmination of everything I enjoy doing and learning. I have found my passion on this earth and after being called to transition out of the military I took my talents to the civilian world to develop solutions to real world problems.

Now here I am, poised to “make a dent in the universe”.
